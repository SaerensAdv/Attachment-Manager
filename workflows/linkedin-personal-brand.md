# Workflow: LinkedIn Personal Brand (Axel)

<!-- deliverable: linkedin-post -->

## Goal

Turn Axel's **own** input into 1 à 3 postklare LinkedIn-concepten in his voice,
so he can build his personal brand on LinkedIn. Google Ads is the core focus;
broader entrepreneurship and marketing themes are allowed when they come from
Axel's own input. Every post is grounded in his real input and his voice
profile — never AI-invented topics. Everything is review-only: Axel reviews,
picks, and posts himself; nothing is published automatically.

## When to use

Axel wants LinkedIn content for his personal brand — either he already has
something to say (a result, an opinion, a lesson, an observation) or he wants to
explore what to post about. (For client-facing organic content use
`workflows/content-production.md`; for paid social use `workflows/ad-creatives.md`.)

## Modes

- **Brain-dump** — Axel supplies the substance up front (a rough note, a voice
  memo transcript, a few bullet points). The team shapes it into 1 à 3 concepts.
- **Brainstorm** — Axel wants to explore. The team first asks a short set of
  clarifying questions and proposes 2–3 distinct angles, and only drafts once
  Axel has supplied the substance. This mode never invents the topic; it helps
  Axel surface his own.

The mode is set from the request (default to brain-dump when Axel already gave
substance; use brainstorm when he asks for ideas or the input is thin).

## Steps

1. Load Axel's voice profile (`knowledge/founder-voice.md`) and the honesty rules
   (`knowledge/agency-foundations.md`). If the voice profile still has
   `[in te vullen uit export]` markers, treat those traits as unknown and lean on
   what is known — never substitute a generic founder voice.
2. Determine the mode (brain-dump or brainstorm) and read Axel's input.
3. **Brainstorm mode only:** ask a short set of clarifying questions and propose
   2–3 distinct angles; wait for Axel's substance before drafting.
4. Choose 1 à 3 angles grounded strictly in Axel's input.
5. Draft each concept in Axel's voice with LinkedIn mechanics: a hook in the
   first line (≤ ~110 chars, no throat-clearing), ~800–1000 chars, short
   paragraphs with white space, exactly one question-CTA, and 3–5 relevant
   hashtags at the end.
6. Never invent facts, figures, cases, client names, or anecdotes. If the input
   is too thin for a credible post, deliver fewer variants and list the missing
   substance to ask Axel — do not fill the gap.
7. **Optional visual (clearly bounded last step):** from an approved/chosen post,
   the Creative Designer can build a LinkedIn carousel or infographic as HTML in
   the mockup-sandbox, exportable to image, using the paid-social creative
   patterns (`knowledge/paid-social-creative.md`). The visual takes its content
   **only** from the chosen post — no new claims.
8. Prepare the human-approval summary; nothing is posted automatically.

## Agents involved

- Orchestrator Agent (routes and briefs)
- Personal Brand Strategist (lead — Axel's ghostwriter)
- Copywriter (content & social specialty — sharpening and hooks)
- Creative Designer (optional visual: carousel / infographic, bounded last step)

> The Humanizer (natural-voice pass) and the QA & Compliance Reviewer run
> automatically as the closing quality gate; they are not listed as team members.

## Required output

Follow `templates/task-output.md`, `knowledge/founder-voice.md`, and
`knowledge/agency-foundations.md`. Must include:

- The mode used and the topic (from Axel's own input)
- Brainstorm questions and angles (in brainstorm mode, before drafting)
- 1 à 3 LinkedIn post concepts, each with hook, body, one question-CTA, and 3–5
  hashtags, plus a one-line rationale — no invented facts or figures
- Any missing substance to ask Axel, if the input was thin
- An optional bounded visual brief (carousel / infographic) from the chosen post
- Human approval required: Axel reviews, picks, and posts himself; nothing is
  published automatically
