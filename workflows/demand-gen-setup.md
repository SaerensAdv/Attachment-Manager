# Workflow: Google Demand Gen Campaign Setup

## Goal

Prepare a complete, implementation-ready Google **Demand Gen** setup for a client — from strategy to a structure a human can review and build — across YouTube (including Shorts), Discover, and Gmail, positioned to create the demand that Search and other channels capture.

## When to use

A client wants to launch Demand Gen, add a demand-creation channel to an existing Google Ads program, or rebuild an underperforming Demand Gen account. Works for both e-commerce and lead generation.

## Steps

1. Understand the business, offer, and the creative assets available.
2. Review the client context (`clients/<client>.md`).
3. Confirm the objective and the client's world (e-commerce or lead generation).
4. Choose the formats to run (in-stream / Shorts / in-feed / Discover / image ads) per `knowledge/demand-gen.md`.
5. Define the campaign structure and settings — lean, focused campaigns (1–2 creatives each), separated by creative/audience/funnel/format, with testing kept separate from scaling.
6. Define audiences and the **critical exclusions** (past purchasers, site visitors, email lists) so cold prospecting does not run as remarketing.
7. Recommend the funnel and destination per world (quiz, VSL→booking, product/collection, advertorial, comparison) with the Landing Page / Web Design Specialist.
8. Set the creative brief (formats, hooks, angles) and hand copy/scripts to the Copywriter and produced visuals/video to the Creative Designer.
9. Define measurement: the conversion event, the conversion window, the ad-event-type check, and the incrementality plan (`knowledge/measurement-reporting.md`).
10. Define the weekly scaling/optimization loop and the winner-scaling rule.
11. Prepare the human approval summary (budget, go-live, tracking).

## Saerens approach

- **Creative is the biggest lever.** A strong hook and angle beat a clever setup; brief creative from real customer and competitor research.
- **Exclusions are non-negotiable.** Without them, Smart Bidding turns a "cold" campaign into remarketing and reports inflated results.
- **Conversion tracking is the go-live gate** — no Demand Gen campaign goes live without trustworthy tracking, full stop.
- **Read the metrics honestly.** Check the click vs engaged-view split and treat Google's prospecting ROAS as an understatement; factor incrementality and LTV before scaling.
- **Test like Meta, then scale winners.** Keep a testing campaign, promote proven creatives, and scale ~20% every 3–5 days while performance holds.

## Agents involved

- Orchestrator Agent (routes and briefs)
- Demand Gen Specialist (strategy, structure, settings, targeting, funnel, scaling, measurement — lead)
- Copywriter (ad copy and video scripts from the creative brief)
- Creative Designer (produced visuals and video from the creative brief)
- Landing Page / Web Design Specialist (funnel destination and message match)
- Analytics & Tracking Specialist (conversion tracking, conversion window, incrementality)

## Required output

Follow the Demand Gen Specialist output format. Must include:

- Strategic summary and market/goal framing (e-commerce vs lead generation)
- Recommended formats and why
- Campaign structure and settings (lean campaigns, testing vs scaling, bidding, budget split, conversion window)
- Audience strategy and the critical exclusions
- Funnel recommendation (ad → landing experience)
- Creative brief (copy/scripts handed to the Copywriter; visuals/video to the Creative Designer)
- Measurement and incrementality (conversion event, ad-event-type check, branded-search and native-tracking checks)
- Scaling and optimization plan
- Risks & dependencies (tracking and creative readiness)
- Open questions / missing information
- Human approval required (budget + go-live)
