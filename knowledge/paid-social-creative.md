# Paid Social & Ad Creative

Standards for paid social strategy on Meta (Facebook & Instagram) — **Meta ads standards** — and for the visual/creative packages that run across Meta and Google — **Ad creative standards**. Strategy sets the funnel, audiences and budget logic; the creative standards govern the assets that fill those placements.


---

## Meta Ads Standards

Baseline standards for how Saerens builds and manages Meta (Facebook & Instagram) advertising. Agents apply these unless a client's context gives a documented reason to deviate. These are agency conventions, not a substitute for current Meta policy — always defer to live Meta Ads rules.

### How Meta differs from Google Ads

- Meta is **demand generation** — it creates and captures interest in feeds — while Google Search captures **existing high intent**. Strategy, creative, and measurement expectations differ accordingly.
- Don't judge Meta purely on last-click conversions; account for its **assist and demand-creation role** alongside Google.

### Account structure

- Structure around the **objective**: separate **prospecting** (new audiences) from **retargeting** (warm audiences and customers).
- For e-commerce, use **catalog / Advantage+ Shopping** campaigns for scale; layer dynamic retargeting where it adds value.
- Keep structure **simple enough to learn**: avoid fragmenting budget across too many ad sets, which starves the learning phase.
- Use **Advantage+ / broad audiences** deliberately, and only with reliable conversion tracking in place.

### Audiences

- Build **custom audiences** (site visitors, customer lists, engagers) and **lookalikes** from high-value sources.
- Apply **exclusions** (e.g. existing customers, recent purchasers) to avoid wasted spend.
- Respect audience size and privacy realities — never invent audience sizes; flag when data is needed.

### Creative

- Creative is the **biggest lever** on Meta. Recommend **multiple distinct angles and hooks**, not minor variations.
- Prioritize **mobile-first, native** formats (vertical video, reels, carousels) suited to placements.
- Lead with a **clear hook in the first seconds/line**, a single message, and one clear call to action.
- Hand actual ad copy to the **Copywriter**; respect brand restrictions and never make unverifiable claims.

### Bidding & budgets

- Choose the objective and bidding approach that fit the goal and available data; conversion optimization needs reliable tracking first.
- Allow campaigns to exit the **learning phase** — avoid over-editing and budget changes that reset learning.
- Recommend budgets against the goal (target ROAS or cost per qualified lead) and flag that **budget changes need client approval**.

### Measurement

- Require a working **Meta Pixel and Conversions API (CAPI)** for reliable signal; align the conversion event with real business value (see `knowledge/measurement-reporting.md`).
- Watch for **iOS / privacy signal loss** and modeled conversions; be transparent about attribution limits.
- For lead gen, weigh **lead quality**, not just volume of form opens.

### Before launch — non-negotiables

- Conversion tracking (Pixel + CAPI) verified for the chosen event.
- Audiences and exclusions defined.
- At least a few distinct creative angles ready (copy via the Copywriter).
- Budget and go-live approved by the client (**human approval required**).

## Ad Creative Standards

Baseline standards for how Saerens builds **paid-ad creatives** (the visual ad together with its copy) for Meta (Facebook & Instagram) and Google (Display, Demand Gen, and RSA text). Agents apply these unless a client's context gives a documented reason to deviate. These are agency conventions, not a substitute for current platform policy — always defer to live Meta and Google Ads rules.

### What an ad creative is

- A creative = a **concept / angle** + the **on-image (or in-video) text** + the **post copy** (primary text, headline, description) + a **format** for a specific placement.
- Always deliver **multiple distinct angles**, not reworded twins. Strong default trio: a **pain point**, a **curiosity / question**, and **social proof**.

### On-image / hook rules

- Keep on-image text **short and scannable**: aim for **≤ ~20 words**, ideally 3–4 elements (logo, headline, one benefit line, one CTA).
- One **clear hook** that stops the scroll; a single message per creative; one clear call to action.
- Lead with the customer's problem or desired outcome — not the agency.

### Copy fields & limits (deliver per creative)

Meta (Feed):
- **Primary text** — front-load the hook; ~125 characters show before "see more".
- **Headline** — ~27–40 characters.
- **Description** — ~30 characters (optional, placement-dependent).

Google:
- **Responsive Search Ads** — headlines ≤ 30 chars, descriptions ≤ 90 chars (defer to the Copywriter's RSA work).
- **Demand Gen / Display** — short headline ≤ 30, long headline ≤ 90, description ≤ 90.

Always note the **character count** next to each line and never exceed platform limits.

### Formats & placements

- Meta Feed: **4:5** (1080×1350). Stories / Reels: **9:16**. Square 1:1 where needed.
- Design **mobile-first** and safe-zone aware (avoid text in the bottom / UI overlap on Stories & Reels).
- Match the creative to the placement; don't reuse a feed image as a story without reframing.

### Brand & honesty

- Follow the client's tone (`clients/<client>.md`) and `knowledge/agency-foundations.md`.
- Use the client's **real offer and proof** only — never invent prices, guarantees, or performance claims (`knowledge/agency-foundations.md`).
- Respect brand restrictions and any claims the client cannot make; flag anything needing client confirmation.

### Message match

- The ad's promise must match the **landing page** it sends to; keep offer, wording, and CTA consistent end-to-end.

### Handoffs

- **Creative direction** (which angles, funnel stage, format strategy) is set with the **Meta Ads Strategist** for paid social.
- **Final visual production** (images / video) is handed to media / video generation; the creative package defines exactly what to produce.
- For anything affecting live spend, route a **QA & Compliance Reviewer** check before human approval.
