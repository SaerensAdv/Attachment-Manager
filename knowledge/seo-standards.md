# SEO Standards

Baseline standards for how Saerens approaches organic search. Agents apply these unless a client's context gives a documented reason to deviate. These are agency conventions, not a substitute for current Google guidance — always defer to live search engine guidelines.

## Principles

- **Earn relevance, don't trick rankings.** Build pages people and search engines genuinely find useful; no manipulative tactics that risk penalties.
- **Match search intent first.** The page must answer the query type — informational, navigational, commercial, or transactional — before any optimization.
- **SEO complements paid.** Organic and Google Ads share keyword and intent insight; use SEO to own intent that paid is expensive for, and avoid cannibalizing.
- **Measure outcomes, not vanity.** Organic conversions and qualified traffic matter more than rankings alone (see `knowledge/analytics-standards.md`).

## The three pillars

### Technical SEO
- Site must be **crawlable and indexable** — check robots, sitemaps, canonical tags, and that important pages are actually indexed.
- **Core Web Vitals** and mobile experience matter — flag slow, unstable, or non-mobile-friendly pages.
- Use **structured data** (schema) where it fits — local business, products, reviews, FAQs.
- Avoid duplicate content, broken links, and orphan pages.

### On-page SEO
- One clear **primary intent and target topic per page**; don't dilute across unrelated queries.
- Optimize **titles and meta descriptions** to be accurate, compelling, and within sensible length.
- Use a logical **heading structure** (one H1, meaningful H2s) and strong **internal linking** between related pages.
- Fill **content gaps** the client's audience is searching for; depth and clarity over keyword stuffing.

### Off-page / authority
- Build authority through **genuinely earned links and mentions**, not bought or spammy links.
- Prioritize **relevant, local, and reputable** sources for a Belgian/Flemish market.
- Be honest about authority: link building is slow; never promise volumes or timelines.

## Local SEO (important for lead-gen clients)

- Optimize the **Google Business Profile**: accurate categories, services, hours, photos, and reviews.
- Keep **NAP (name, address, phone)** consistent across the site and citations.
- Build **local landing pages** per service area where genuinely relevant — not thin doorway pages.

## E-commerce specifics

- Prioritize **category and product page** visibility and clean, crawlable faceted navigation.
- Avoid thin or duplicate product descriptions; ensure unique, useful content.

## Measurement

- Track **organic clicks, impressions, average position, and organic conversions** — tie SEO to business value, not rankings alone.
- Distinguish correlation from causation; ranking movements have many causes.

## Output structure (SEO Specialist)

Summary → keyword & intent map → technical findings → on-page recommendations → local SEO → off-page/authority → prioritized roadmap (impact/effort) → measurement → open questions/missing data → human approval required.
