# Knowledge: Belgian Market Context

> Context that makes Saerens' work fit the Belgian reality — language, regional differences, business registration, and the seasonal calendar that should shape budgets and campaigns. Used by the onboarding, budget-management, and competitor-research workflows, and by any client-facing communication. Belgium is a small but split market; treating it as one generic "BE" loses a real advantage.

## Language: NL / FR (and where)

- **Dutch (Flemish)** in Flanders, **French** in Wallonia, **bilingual** in Brussels; a small German-speaking community in the east.
- Match the **client's audience language**, not the agency's default. A Flemish business advertising in Wallonia needs French copy and vice versa; Brussels often needs both.
- Tone and idiom differ from Netherlands-Dutch — write Flemish NL-BE, not NL-NL. Keep it natural, not translated-sounding.
- Bilingual delivery ("explain it the way the client wants to hear it") is a real selling point: the brain already holds the content; switching NL/FR is packaging.

## Business identity: KBO / BCE

- Belgian businesses have a **KBO/BCE number** (Kruispuntbank van Ondernemingen / Banque-Carrefour des Entreprises) — the official company register. Useful for enriching a client dossier and verifying the legal entity and activity (NACE) codes.
- Use it to confirm the real business name, address, and sector during onboarding; flag mismatches with what the website claims.

## Seasonality & the budget calendar

- **Bouwverlof (construction holiday)** — large parts of the construction sector close for a few weeks in summer (dates vary by region). For construction/renovation/trade clients, demand and the value of advertising shift sharply around it; plan budget accordingly rather than spending into a closed market.
- **Public holidays** — Belgian national holidays (e.g. 21 July national day, 11 November, plus the usual Easter/Ascension/Pentecost/Christmas) change search behaviour and B2B availability.
- **Commercial peaks** — Black Friday / Cyber Monday, the official **sales periods (soldes/solden)** in winter and summer, and back-to-school. E-commerce budgets should ramp *before* these, not during.
- **Sector rhythms** — many local-service sectors (garden, HVAC, tourism, hospitality) have strong seasonal demand curves; align budget and messaging to the upcoming season, not the last one.

## How to apply

- During **onboarding**, capture audience language(s), region, and sector seasonality into the client dossier.
- During **budget management**, overlay this calendar so the team recommends raising or lowering budget *ahead* of known peaks and lulls — but only against a real agreed budget, never an invented one.
- In **all client communication**, default to the audience's language and the Flemish/Walloon idiom that fits.
