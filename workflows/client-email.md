# Workflow: Client Email Draft

## Goal

Draft a clear, on-brand client email — for example sharing results, proposing a change, requesting information, or following up — that a Saerens team member can review, adjust, and send.

## When to use

Any time a client communication needs to be written: report cover notes, recommendations, requests for budget or assets, onboarding messages, or follow-ups.

## Steps

1. Identify the client and the purpose of the email (`clients/<client>.md`).
2. Confirm the key message and any data or decisions it must contain.
3. Match the client's tone of voice and Saerens' overall voice (`knowledge/tone-of-voice.md`).
4. Keep it clear and concise: lead with the point, then the detail, then a clear ask or next step.
5. Be transparent — no spin, no overpromising, no surprises.
6. Flag anything that needs internal confirmation before sending (numbers, commitments).

## Agents involved

- Orchestrator Agent (routes and briefs)
- Reporting Specialist or relevant specialist (for the substance)
- Copywriter (for tone and clarity)

## Required output

Use `templates/client-email.md`. Must include:

- Subject line
- Greeting
- The main message (clear and to the point)
- Supporting detail or data (only what's confirmed)
- A clear ask or next step
- Sign-off
- Internal notes: anything to confirm before sending
