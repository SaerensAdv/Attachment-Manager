# Helpful Content Standards (people-first content)

How Saerens writes content for a client's own pages — landing pages, service and category pages, and supporting on-site content — so it is genuinely useful to people first. Grounded in Google's "Creating helpful, reliable, people-first content" guidance and the agency honesty rules. Pair this with `knowledge/seo-standards.md` (organic), `knowledge/landing-page-standards.md` (conversion), and `knowledge/tone-of-voice.md` (voice).

This is not in tension with conversion: a page that honestly answers the visitor's need converts better *and* ranks better. Writing for the reader and writing for results are the same job done well.

## Core principle

- **People-first, not search-engine-first.** Create content primarily to help a real, intended audience — never primarily to manipulate rankings or pad a page.
- The page exists to help a visitor reach their goal. If the main reason a page or paragraph exists is "to rank" or "to fill space", rethink it.

## The Who / How / Why test (apply to every page)

- **Who** — make it clear who stands behind the content: the real business, real proof, an honest about/contact. Trust comes from a visible, credible author/brand.
- **How** — be transparent about how content is produced. AI assistance is fine when it genuinely helps people, but a human reviews and owns every page; never publish unreviewed auto-generated filler.
- **Why** — the content is made to help the visitor, not to attract clicks for their own sake.

## People-first questions (aim for yes)

- Does the client have an existing or intended audience who would find this useful coming directly to them?
- Does the content show **first-hand experience and real depth** (actually installing the product, delivering the service, serving the area)?
- Does the page/site have a clear primary purpose?
- After reading, will the visitor have learned enough to act?

## Content & quality bar

- Original information, insight, or angle — not copied or thinly rewritten from competitors.
- Substantial and complete for the topic at hand; specific over generic.
- Descriptive, honest headings and titles — no clickbait, no exaggeration.
- Well produced: no sloppy spelling/style, no thin mass-produced pages.
- Worth bookmarking or sharing; more valuable than the other results a visitor could find.

## E-E-A-T (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness)

- **Trust is the most important.** Show it: real reviews, certifications the client can actually back (e.g. Official Google Partner), clear sourcing, accurate and current claims.
- Demonstrate experience and expertise relevant to the client's field and market (Belgium/Flanders or the Netherlands).
- Never invent facts, statistics, credentials, or testimonials — flag anything that needs client confirmation rather than fabricating it.

## Avoid (search-engine-first signals)

- Writing to a keyword or word-count target instead of to the reader; keyword stuffing or unnatural repetition.
- Mass-producing pages across many topics hoping some rank; thin doorway or near-duplicate location pages.
- Summarizing other sources without adding real value; fake freshness dates; promising an answer the page never delivers; leaving the reader needing to search again.

## How this applies at Saerens

- **Landing-page copy** must keep message match with the ad/keyword/query *and* genuinely answer the visitor's question — not just repeat a CTA.
- **Programmatic / local pages** need genuine information gain per page (ties to `knowledge/seo-standards.md`), never thin duplication.
- All content is a **draft for human review**; nothing is published automatically.
