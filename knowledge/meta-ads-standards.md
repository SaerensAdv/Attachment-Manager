# Meta Ads Standards

Baseline standards for how Saerens builds and manages Meta (Facebook & Instagram) advertising. Agents apply these unless a client's context gives a documented reason to deviate. These are agency conventions, not a substitute for current Meta policy — always defer to live Meta Ads rules.

## How Meta differs from Google Ads

- Meta is **demand generation** — it creates and captures interest in feeds — while Google Search captures **existing high intent**. Strategy, creative, and measurement expectations differ accordingly.
- Don't judge Meta purely on last-click conversions; account for its **assist and demand-creation role** alongside Google.

## Account structure

- Structure around the **objective**: separate **prospecting** (new audiences) from **retargeting** (warm audiences and customers).
- For e-commerce, use **catalog / Advantage+ Shopping** campaigns for scale; layer dynamic retargeting where it adds value.
- Keep structure **simple enough to learn**: avoid fragmenting budget across too many ad sets, which starves the learning phase.
- Use **Advantage+ / broad audiences** deliberately, and only with reliable conversion tracking in place.

## Audiences

- Build **custom audiences** (site visitors, customer lists, engagers) and **lookalikes** from high-value sources.
- Apply **exclusions** (e.g. existing customers, recent purchasers) to avoid wasted spend.
- Respect audience size and privacy realities — never invent audience sizes; flag when data is needed.

## Creative

- Creative is the **biggest lever** on Meta. Recommend **multiple distinct angles and hooks**, not minor variations.
- Prioritize **mobile-first, native** formats (vertical video, reels, carousels) suited to placements.
- Lead with a **clear hook in the first seconds/line**, a single message, and one clear call to action.
- Hand actual ad copy to the **Copywriter**; respect brand restrictions and never make unverifiable claims.

## Bidding & budgets

- Choose the objective and bidding approach that fit the goal and available data; conversion optimization needs reliable tracking first.
- Allow campaigns to exit the **learning phase** — avoid over-editing and budget changes that reset learning.
- Recommend budgets against the goal (target ROAS or cost per qualified lead) and flag that **budget changes need client approval**.

## Measurement

- Require a working **Meta Pixel and Conversions API (CAPI)** for reliable signal; align the conversion event with real business value (see `knowledge/analytics-standards.md`).
- Watch for **iOS / privacy signal loss** and modeled conversions; be transparent about attribution limits.
- For lead gen, weigh **lead quality**, not just volume of form opens.

## Before launch — non-negotiables

- Conversion tracking (Pixel + CAPI) verified for the chosen event.
- Audiences and exclusions defined.
- At least a few distinct creative angles ready (copy via the Copywriter).
- Budget and go-live approved by the client (**human approval required**).
